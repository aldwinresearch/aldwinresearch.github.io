Aldwin Research

Interdisciplinary research at the intersection of life, computation, and environment

We explore complex systems through the lens of biology, computer science, atmospheric science, and robotics. Our work bridges scales from molecular mechanisms to planetary dynamics, using computational approaches to understand and engineer living systems.

Research Areas

Computational Biology

Modeling biological systems, from protein folding dynamics to ecosystem interactions. We develop algorithms for genomic analysis, phylogenetic reconstruction, and systems biology approaches to understand cellular networks.

Bio-Inspired Robotics

Engineering autonomous systems that learn from natural phenomena. Our robots incorporate biological principles of adaptation, swarm intelligence, and morphological computation for environmental monitoring and exploration.

Atmospheric Modeling

Computational studies of atmospheric chemistry, climate dynamics, and air quality. We integrate biological and chemical processes to understand how living systems both respond to and influence atmospheric conditions.

Machine Learning for Earth Systems

Applying neural networks and statistical methods to environmental data. Our models predict ecosystem responses to climate change and optimize robotic sensor networks for environmental monitoring.
